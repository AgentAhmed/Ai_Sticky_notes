India is the world's most populous country with over 1.4 billion people as of 2024
India has 28 states and 8 union territories, with New Delhi as the national capital
Hindi and English are the official languages, but India recognizes 22 official languages in total
India is the birthplace of four major world religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism
The Indian economy is the world's fifth-largest by nominal GDP and third-largest by purchasing power parity
India gained independence from British rule on August 15, 1947, led by figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru
The Taj Mahal in Agra is one of the Seven Wonders of the World and India's most famous landmark
India is a major IT and software services hub, with cities like Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Pune being tech centers
The monsoon season (June-September) is crucial for India's agriculture, providing about 70% of annual rainfall
India has a rich cultural heritage including classical dance forms like Bharatanatyam, diverse cuisines, and the world's largest film industry (Bollywood)
